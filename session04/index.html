Mushrooms

Mushrooms are fascinating organisms that bridge worlds between forests and food. Their forms and functions invite careful observation and design thinking. Visit Wikipedia for broad overviews.

“The study of fungi reveals the hidden networks sustaining life.”

More

Mushrooms are the fruiting bodies of fungi—they release spores from gills, pores, or teeth, while the underground mycelium threads digest wood and leaf litter, recycle nutrients, and even form partnerships with trees. From the iconic Amanita muscaria to the edible Pleurotus ostreatus, each species has distinct shapes, colors, and habitats. For color references and identification basics, see the Mushroom Color Atlas.
